Chicken Katsu

Description

Chicken Katsu is a simple Japanese dish made of deep-fried chicken coated in panko (japanese breadcrumbs).

It can be served with white rice or shredded lettuce and tonkatsu sauce, which you can buy at Asian supermarkets.

Ingredients(4 servings)

  • 2 skinless, boneless chickin breasts
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 large egg, beaten
  • 150g panko bread crumbs
  • vegetable oil for frying

Steps

  1. Gather all ingridients.
  2. Butterfly the chicken breasts.
  3. Season chickine breasts on both sides with salt and pepper.
  4. Place flour, beaten eggs, and panko into separate dishes.
  5. Coat chicken breasts in flour, shaking off any excess.
  6. Dip into egg, and then press onto panko crumbs until well coated on both sides.
  7. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Place chicken in the hot oil.
  8. Fry until golden brown, 3 or 4 minutes on each side. Transfer to a plate with the paper towel. Let the oil drain.
  9. Serve and enjoy!
