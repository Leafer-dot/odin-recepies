Chicken Katsu

Description

Chicken Katsu is a simple Japanese dish made of deep-fried chicken coated in panko (japanese breadcrumbs).

It can be served with white rice or shredded lettuce and tonkatsu sauce, which you can buy at Asian supermarkets.

Ingredients (4 servings)

  • 2 skinless, boneless chickin breasts
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 large egg, beaten
  • 150g panko bread crumbs
  • vegetable oil for frying

Steps

  1. Butterfly the chicken breasts.
  2. Season chicken breasts on both sides.
  3. Put the flour, beaten eggs, and panko into three separate bowls.
  4. Dredge the chicken breasts in the flour and shake off the extra.
  5. Dip the chicken into the egg, then coat it with panko until both sides are fully covered.
  6. Heat oil in a large pan over medium-high heat and add the chicken.
  7. Cook until golden brown, about 3 to 4 minutes per side.
  8. Move to a plate lined with paper towels and let the excess oil drain.
  9. Enjoy!
